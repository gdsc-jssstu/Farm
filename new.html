Personal Data Awareness and Statistics in India

It's important to be aware of how your personal data is being collected, used, and shared. Here's some information and statistics about personal data in India:

What is personal data?

Personal data is any information that can be used to identify a specific individual. This can include things like your name, address, phone number, email address, and even your browsing history and location data.

How is personal data collected in India?

Personal data can be collected in a variety of ways, including through online forms, cookies, and tracking technologies. It can also be collected offline, such as through credit application forms or government documents. In India, The Personal Data Protection Bill, 2019 was passed in December 2019, which will soon be enforced as a law.

Data Privacy in India

India has recently passed the Personal Data Protection Bill, 2019, which aims to regulate the collection, storage, and use of personal data by companies and organizations. The bill establishes a Data Protection Authority to enforce compliance and protect individuals' rights to their personal data.

According to a report by the Data Security Council of India, the number of data breaches in India increased by 27% in 2019, with financial services and the government sector being the most affected.

Statistics on personal data in India

  • According to a survey conducted by the Internet and Mobile Association of India (IAMAI) in 2019, 81% of internet users in India are concerned about their personal data being misused.
  • The number of data breaches in India has increased by over 150% in the last two years, according to a report by the Data Security Council of India (DSCI).
  • A report by the Centre for Internet and Society (CIS) found that out of the top 100 websites in India, only 6 had a privacy policy that was compliant with international standards.

Protecting your personal data

It's important to take steps to protect your personal data. Here are a few things you can do:

  • Be cautious about sharing personal information online, especially on social media.
  • Check the privacy policies of websites and apps before using them.
  • Use a VPN when accessing the internet on public Wi-Fi.
  • Keep your software and devices up to date with the latest security updates.

By being aware of how your personal data is being collected and used, and taking steps to protect it, you can help keep your information safe.
